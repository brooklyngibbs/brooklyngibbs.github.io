Roku City Is Open

The most-watched screen in America is the one nobody's watching. Roku City — the idle skyline that millions of TVs fall asleep to — becomes a place you can actually visit.

Brand
Roku
Surface
The idle screen

Speculative concept

The Insight

Roku City is the rarest thing a brand can own: a piece of culture people are fond of by accident.

Nobody was told to love the Roku screensaver. They just did. People screenshot it, name the little shops in it, post "Roku City appreciation," wonder what's happening in the windows. It's beloved precisely because it asks for nothing — pure ambient comfort at the end of a scroll.

But that affection is latent. It has nowhere to go. The campaign doesn't manufacture love for Roku City — it gives the love that already exists somewhere to live. You stop falling asleep to the city and start visiting it.

Themes & Pillars

The City Comes Alive

The screensaver stops being wallpaper and starts being a place with weather, seasons, and a calendar. It reacts to what's happening — so the thing you used to ignore becomes the thing you check.

Skyline Events

The city lights up for premieres, finales, and holidays. A big premiere paints the skyline its title's colors; a finale throws fireworks. Appointment viewing — for a screensaver.

Neighborhoods

Districts themed to genre and season. A horror alley in October, a rom-com boulevard in February, the stadium on game night. "What's playing in Roku City tonight?"

Citizens, Not Viewers

The people already screenshotting the city are the campaign. Give them tools and rewards, and passive affection turns into participation — and into reach Roku doesn't have to buy.

Found a Secret Window

Easter eggs hidden in the skyline and a lightweight builder — add your shop, name your block. Reward the curiosity people already bring to the screen.

The Night Shift

The city leaks into the real world: projected on buildings, a walkable pop-up you can step inside, merch of your favorite block. The screensaver goes IRL.

Why It Works

01

It converts love that's already there

No persuasion needed. The audience is pre-sold — the work just builds the door they've been knocking on.

02

Ad inventory that feels like a place

Neighborhoods are native brand integrations that read as part of the city, not an interruption — the rare ad people seek out.

03

It gives Roku an identity beyond hardware

Apple has design. Roku has a city everyone secretly likes. This makes that the brand, not the dongle.

In One Line

A screensaver is just a city waiting for the lights to come on.
