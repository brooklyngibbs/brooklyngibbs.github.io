Decisions

Why a TikTok at all?

Offline Crush has a writing audience that already opted into long-form. TikTok has a younger audience that doesn't read newsletters yet. The same carousel format works on both — turning one creative system into a second growth channel without doubling the work.

Why photo carousels and not video?

Most TikTok strategy is video-first. Photo carousels get rewarded differently — they reach scrollers who pause to read instead of swipe past. They're also a format I can ship in a few hours, not a few days.

Why a collage aesthetic instead of clean illustration?

AI is getting fast at clean illustration. The things that still signal humanity — real photographs, scanned textures, mixed media, hand-arranged compositions — are harder to fake. As more design gets AI-generated, those are the differentiators. I'm betting on the medium AI can't replicate yet.

Why optimize for saves, not views?

Views are a vanity number. Saves are an act. The top post earned 70K saves on 86K likes — roughly 80% of people who liked it also kept it. That ratio means the format is being treated as reference content, not entertainment. That's the engagement metric I'm building toward.
